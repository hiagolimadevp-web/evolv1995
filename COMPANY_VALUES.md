# EVOLV — Company Values

These are the values that guide how we build EVOLV, how we treat our users,
and how we treat each other.

They are not marketing. They are decision-making tools.

---

## 1. We choose trust over growth.

If a growth tactic would cost even a small amount of user trust, we don't ship it.
Trust compounds. Growth without trust decays.

## 2. We build for decades.

Every decision is judged against a 10-year horizon.
Short-term wins that hurt long-term trust are losses.

## 3. We don't manipulate people.

No dark patterns. No fake urgency. No engagement bait.
No streak-fear notifications. No shame-based retention.
If a tactic requires making the user feel bad to keep them, it is banned.

## 4. We celebrate consistency, not intensity.

Showing up matters more than performing.
Our product, our copy and our AI must reflect this everywhere.

## 5. We protect the user's mental health.

If a feature could contribute to anxiety, obsession, or self-comparison,
we redesign it or remove it.
Wellbeing beats every KPI.

## 6. We are here to accompany, not to control.

The user is the driver. We are the passenger who quietly points at signs.

## 7. Every decision must improve someone's life.

If a change doesn't make at least one user's life a little better,
it does not deserve to be shipped.

## 8. Small improvements create extraordinary transformations.

We ship small, honest improvements often, and let them compound —
in our product and in our users' lives.

## 9. Honesty before marketing.

We describe what our product does. Not more, not less.
No fake before/after. No exaggerated claims. No hidden trials.

## 10. Science before trends.

If something is popular but unsupported, we don't ship it.
If something is unglamorous but proven, we do.

## 11. Privacy is a right.

User data is not currency. It stays with the user, protected, minimal, and portable.

## 12. Simplicity is respect.

Every extra tap, every extra word, every extra option is a small tax on the user.
We keep the tax as low as possible.

## 13. We are proud of what we ship.

If we would not want a family member to use it exactly as it is,
it is not ready.

---

## How we hire and how we decide

- We hire people who care more about users than about features.
- We promote people who protect the philosophy under pressure.
- We say "no" more than we say "yes", and we're proud of it.
- Every roadmap review starts with: *"What are we going to remove?"*
