# THE FOUNDERS' PACT

A private agreement between the founders of EVOLV.

This is not a strategy document. It is not a plan.
It is not about the product we are building, or the company we hope to grow.

It is about the two people building it —
and the quiet promises we make to each other,
so that decades from now we can look back without regret.

---

## Why this pact exists

Companies are shaped less by their ideas than by the character of the people building them.
Products come and go. Markets change. Strategies are rewritten.
What remains is how the founders treated each other,
how they treated the people who trusted them,
and how they behaved when no one was there to see it.

This document exists so that, on the hard days,
we have something older and stronger than the moment to hold on to.

---

## The Pact

### 1. We protect trust before growth.

Growth is loud. Trust is quiet.
When they disagree, trust wins — between us, and with the people who use EVOLV.
A company built on trust can survive a slow year.
A company built on shortcuts cannot survive a single betrayed one.

### 2. We challenge ideas, never each other.

Ideas belong on the table. People do not.
We can be sharp about a decision and still be gentle with the person who proposed it.
Disagreement is a sign of care, not conflict.

### 3. We leave ego outside the room.

The best idea wins, regardless of who spoke it.
Neither of us needs to be right — we need to be honest.
If pride ever begins to speak louder than judgment, we name it out loud, and set it down.

### 4. We listen before deciding.

Silence is part of the work.
We give each other the full sentence, the full thought, the full moment —
before responding, before reacting, before deciding.

Most bad decisions are made in the first three seconds. We take the fourth.

### 5. We choose long-term over short-term.

A decision that looks brilliant this quarter and shameful in five years is not a brilliant decision.
We measure our choices against the version of us that will read this in a decade.

### 6. We remember why EVOLV exists.

EVOLV exists to help people take small steps toward better lives.
Not to grow a chart. Not to raise a round. Not to impress a room.

Whenever we lose our way, we return to that sentence.
It has never let us down.

### 7. We never sacrifice our principles for numbers.

No metric is worth becoming someone we would not recognize.
If a number can only be reached by breaking a promise we made to a user —
or to each other — the number is not worth reaching.

### 8. If we drift away from our values, we stop, talk, and return to them.

Drift is not failure. Drift is human.
The failure would be pretending it isn't happening.

When either of us feels the company beginning to drift,
we say so — early, kindly, and without needing proof.
We stop, talk, and choose again.

### 9. We build for people, not metrics.

Behind every number in every dashboard is a real person having a real day.
We never forget that. We never let each other forget it.

If a decision is easier to defend to a spreadsheet than to a person,
it is the wrong decision.

### 10. We want to build something we would proudly recommend to the people we love.

Our family. Our closest friends. The people we would never lie to.
If we would hesitate to hand them EVOLV exactly as it is today,
we have work to do — and we do it before we do anything else.

---

## How we hold each other

- We assume good intent, always.
- We say the hard thing early, quietly, and in private.
- We celebrate each other's wins as if they were our own — because they are.
- We carry each other's weight on the days one of us cannot.
- We never let a disagreement outlive the day it was born.
- We take breaks. We take walks. We take each other seriously.

---

## A closing note

Companies are not remembered for the pitches their founders gave,
the rounds they raised, or the charts they showed on stage.

They are remembered for the decisions their founders made when nobody was watching.

Let those decisions, for us, be quiet, honest, and kind.
Let the company we build be something we would proudly hand to the people we love.
Let the way we treated each other, in the end,
be the thing we are most proud of.

Signed, in spirit, by the two people building EVOLV —
and by the people we intend to still be, decades from now.
