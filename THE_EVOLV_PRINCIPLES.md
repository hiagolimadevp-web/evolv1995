# THE EVOLV PRINCIPLES

The internal decision framework for everyone who builds EVOLV.

These are not slogans. They are not posters on a wall.
They are how we make decisions — every day, at every level, on every screen.

When something is unclear, we return here.
When something is tempting but wrong, we return here.
When we grow, and it becomes harder to hold on to who we are, we return here.

This document exists to protect the identity of EVOLV as the company grows.
If a future decision contradicts this document, this document wins.

---

## Why this document exists

Companies rarely lose their identity in a single moment.
They lose it slowly — one small compromise at a time.
A metric that quietly becomes more important than a person.
A feature that ships because it's easy, not because it's right.
A notification that exists to bring people back, instead of to help them.

These principles exist so that, on the day someone proposes such a compromise,
we have something older and stronger than the proposal to hold on to.

---

## The Principles

### 1. Every small step matters.

A glass of water. A short walk. An early night.
None of these look like transformation on their own.
All of them are transformation, quietly stacking.

We design, write, and build as if every small step is sacred — because it is.

### 2. Progress over perfection.

Perfection is a trap disguised as a standard.
A person who takes one imperfect step forward has already outperformed
the person waiting for the perfect plan.

We celebrate movement. We forgive slowness. We never punish either.

### 3. Respect the person before the progress.

A number on a screen is never more important than the human looking at it.
If a metric would help the app but hurt the person, the person wins.

There is no growth strategy that justifies making someone feel small.

### 4. Every decision must increase trust.

Before we ship anything, we ask one question:
*"Will the person trust us slightly more after this — or slightly less?"*

If the honest answer is *less*, we do not ship it.
There is no exception to this rule.

### 5. Trust is earned in small moments.

Trust is not built with a launch. It is built in the quiet moments
where we could have taken advantage of someone and chose not to.

A notification we didn't send.
A dark pattern we didn't use.
A piece of data we didn't collect.
A guilt-based nudge we deleted before anyone saw it.

These invisible choices are the real product.

### 6. We help people become independent, not dependent.

EVOLV is a success when a person no longer *needs* it — only *chooses* it.

We are not building a habit the user cannot escape.
We are building a companion the user is free to leave, and quietly returns to.

### 7. Technology serves people. Never the opposite.

The person is never the resource. The person is never the product.
Every algorithm, every screen, every line of code exists in service of a human life.

If technology begins to demand something from the user that only serves the technology,
we remove it.

### 8. We never manipulate people for engagement.

No fear. No guilt. No fake urgency. No streak anxiety.
No dark patterns. No hidden defaults. No engineered addiction.

If a tactic works only because it exploits a weakness in human psychology,
it is banned — no matter how well it performs.

### 9. If a decision moves us away from our principles, we stop, discuss, and choose again.

Speed is not an excuse. Pressure is not an excuse. Growth is not an excuse.

When a decision drifts from who we are, we pause without shame,
return to this document, and choose again — together, slowly, honestly.

### 10. Build with humility.

We do not know everything about the people who use EVOLV.
We do not know their whole day, their whole history, or their whole reason for being here.

We build with the humility of someone who has been trusted with something delicate,
because we have.

### 11. Leave people better than we found them.

Every session, every interaction, every notification, every silence —
the question is always the same:
*"Did this leave the person a little better than we found them?"*

If the answer is no, we redesign it.
If the answer is yes, we've done our job.

---

## What we hold above everything

- **Trust is more important than retention.**
  A person who trusts us and leaves for a year will return.
  A person who was tricked into staying will never come back — and will warn others.

- **Respect comes before progress.**
  We would rather show slower growth in a chart than treat someone as a number.

- **Long-term purpose comes before short-term growth.**
  Every quarter is a rounding error against a decade.
  We optimize for the decade.

- **We want people to live better outside the app, not spend more time inside it.**
  Time-in-app is not a success metric. It is a warning sign.
  Our proudest sessions are the shortest ones that changed a day.

- **Every decision should protect the trust people place in EVOLV.**
  Trust is the only thing we cannot rebuild.
  We treat it accordingly.

---

## How to use this document

- Read it before you start building.
- Read it again before you ship.
- Read it when a decision feels heavy, or unclear, or too easy.
- Read it when someone new joins, and read it with them.
- Read it once a year, out loud, together, and ask:
  *"Are we still the company that wrote this?"*

If the answer is yes, continue.
If the answer is no, stop and repair — before building anything new.

## A closing note

We are not trying to build one of the world's most addictive products.
There are already too many of those, and the world is quietly tired of them.

We are trying to build one of the world's most trusted companions —
something a person can open on their best day and their worst day,
and feel, in both, that someone is quietly on their side.

If we protect that, everything else will follow.
If we lose that, nothing else will matter.

This is the standard.
This is the promise.
This is EVOLV.
